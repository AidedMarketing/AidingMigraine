## Validated Clinical Thresholds

### Threshold #1: 5+ hPa Drop in 24 Hours ‚úÖ

**Validation Level:** STRONG

**Studies Supporting:**
1. **Kimoto et al. (2011)** - Internal Medicine
   - 28 patients, prospective study
   - 64% reported weather-related migraines
   - **Finding:** Frequency of migraine increased when pressure dropped >5 hPa from one day to next
   - **Significance:** p < 0.05

2. **Multiple 2024-2025 reviews** confirm this threshold

**Clinical Interpretation:**
- Drop of 0.15 inHg = 5 hPa = 5 mbar
- Most commonly cited threshold in literature
- Well-established in clinical practice

**Recommendation:** ‚úÖ **KEEP 5 hPa / 24h threshold**

**Sources:**
- [Kimoto et al. 2011 - PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21921370/)
- [Kimoto Study Full Text](https://www.jstage.jst.go.jp/article/internalmedicine/50/18/50_18_1923/_article/-char/ja/)

---

### Threshold #2: Absolute Pressure 1003-1007 hPa ‚úÖ

**Validation Level:** STRONG

**Primary Study: Okuma et al. (2015)** - SpringerPlus

**Method:**
- 34 patients (31 female, 3 male)
- Mean age: 32 ¬± 6.7 years
- 22 with aura, 12 without aura
- Compared pressure at migraine onset vs standard 1013 hPa

**Findings:**
- **23.5% of migraines** occurred at 1005-1007 hPa
- **26.5% of migraines** occurred at 1003-1005 hPa
- Combined: **50% of migraines in 1003-1007 hPa range**
- This is 6-10 hPa below standard pressure (1013 hPa)
- Significantly higher than controls (p < 0.05)

**Clinical Interpretation:**
- Absolute pressure level matters, not just changes
- Low pressure systems are high-risk periods
- 6-10 hPa below standard = trigger zone

**Recommendation:** ‚úÖ **ADD absolute pressure monitoring**
- Warn when pressure drops below 1007 hPa
- High alert when pressure < 1005 hPa
- Track personal threshold (varies by individual)

**Sources:**
- [Okuma et al. 2015 - PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4684554/)
- [Okuma et al. - PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26702379/)
- [Okuma et al. - SpringerPlus Full Text](https://springerplus.springeropen.com/articles/10.1186/s40064-015-1592-4)

---

### Threshold #3: Rapid Pressure Changes ‚ö†Ô∏è

**Validation Level:** MODERATE (Needs Refinement)

**Current Implementation:** 3 hPa in 3 hours

**What Research Actually Says:**

1. **General Principle Validated:**
   - Rapid pressure drops trigger trigeminal nerve activation
   - Faster changes = stronger effect
   - Storm systems (20-30 mbar drops in hours) are major triggers

2. **Specific 3h/3hPa NOT Found in Literature:**
   - No studies specifically cite "3 hPa in 3 hours"
   - Most studies use daily (24h) or multi-day windows
   - Rapid = "within hours" but exact threshold varies

3. **What We Know:**
   - **Okuma 2015:** Mentions "rapid reduction" activates trigeminal afferents
   - **Storm research:** 20-30 mbar drops in "just hours" (not specified)
   - **Experimental:** One study changed pressure 40 hPa in 8 minutes (artificial)

**Recommendation:** ‚ö†Ô∏è **REVISE rapid change detection**

**Proposed Approach:**
1. **Keep concept** of rapid change detection (clinically valid)
2. **Revise threshold** to be more conservative:
   - **Option A:** 5+ hPa in any 6-hour window (more conservative)
   - **Option B:** Rate-based: 1+ hPa per hour sustained over 3+ hours
   - **Option C:** Percentile-based: Changes in top 10% for location
3. **Label as "rapid" not "3-hour specific"**
4. **Make user-adjustable** based on personal sensitivity

**Sources:**
- [Okuma et al. 2015](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4684554/)
- [Barometric Pressure Relief Guide](https://sleepandsinuscenters.com/blog/barometric-pressure-headaches-and-sinus-relief-causes-treatment)

## Additional Research Insights

### Individual Variation

**Key Finding:** Not all migraine patients are pressure-sensitive

**Statistics:**
- ~50% of migraine patients report weather as trigger (Kelman 2007)
- 64% in Kimoto study (2011)
- ~20% overall population effect (2025 reviews)
- Individual thresholds vary significantly

**Implication:** Need personalized threshold learning

**Source:** [Current Pain and Headache Reports 2024](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11916-024-01216-8)

---

### Direction of Change

**Drops > Rises:** Pressure decreases are stronger triggers than increases

**Research Support:**
- Kimoto 2011: Frequency increased with drops, decreased with rises
- Okuma 2015: Focused on low pressure (drops from standard)
- General consensus: Falling pressure = higher risk

**Mechanism:**
- Trigeminal nerve activation
- Sinus pressure differential
- Arterial blood pressure changes

---

### Lag Time

**Finding:** Migraines may occur 12-48 hours after pressure change

**Evidence:**
- Kimoto 2011: Compared pressure 1 day before and 2 days after
- Pressure changes may precede migraine by hours to days
- Not immediate trigger for most patients

**Implication:** ‚úÖ **Our ¬±12h correlation window is appropriate**

### 3. Revise Rapid Change Detection ‚ö†Ô∏è

**Option A - Conservative (Recommended):**
```javascript
// REVISED - More conservative, better evidence base
const RAPID_CHANGE_THRESHOLD = 5;  // hPa
const RAPID_CHANGE_WINDOW = 6;     // hours (not 3)

function detectRapidChanges(hourlyReadings) {
    const rapidChanges = [];

    // Check 6-hour sliding windows
    for (let i = 0; i < hourlyReadings.length - 6; i++) {
        const window = hourlyReadings.slice(i, i + 7); // 6-hour window = 7 readings
        const pressures = window.map(r => r.pressure);
        const maxChange = Math.max(...pressures) - Math.min(...pressures);

        if (maxChange >= RAPID_CHANGE_THRESHOLD) {
            rapidChanges.push({
                startTime: window[0].time,
                endTime: window[6].time,
                change: maxChange,
                hoursWindow: 6,
                type: 'rapid-change' // Not "3-hour" specific
            });
        }
    }

    return rapidChanges;
}
```
